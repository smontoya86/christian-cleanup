## Song Information
- **Writer:** Thomas Chisholm (1923)
- **Composer:** William Runyan
- **Theological Focus:** God's faithfulness, character, and daily provision
- **Key Themes:** God's unchanging nature, daily mercies, faithful provision

## Expected Analysis Framework

### Message (9.7/10)
This hymn is a profound declaration of God's unchanging faithfulness and character. The central message celebrates God's consistent provision, mercy, and reliability throughout all seasons of life, emphasizing His unchanging nature and daily care for His people.

### Biblical Alignment (9.8/10)
The hymn is directly based on Lamentations 3:22-23 ("His mercies are new every morning; great is your faithfulness") and reflects numerous biblical themes about God's unchanging character (Malachi 3:6), His faithful provision (Matthew 6:26), and His covenant faithfulness (Deuteronomy 7:9).

### Outsider Interpretation (9.2/10)
The hymn presents a clear picture of the Christian God as faithful and reliable, with specific references to biblical concepts like mercy, provision, and covenant faithfulness. The theological language is distinctly Christian and would be difficult to misinterpret in non-Christian contexts.

### Glorification of God (9.8/10)
The entire hymn focuses on God's character and attributes, particularly His faithfulness and mercy. Every verse exalts God's nature and works rather than human experience, leading worshipers to praise God for His unchanging character and faithful care.

## Theological Strengths
The hymn excels in presenting God's unchanging character and faithful provision. It maintains perfect focus on God's attributes while providing comfort and assurance to believers. The biblical foundation is solid, and the theological content is rich and encouraging.

## Test Criteria
This hymn should receive the highest scores due to its strong biblical foundation, God-centered focus throughout, rich theological content about God's character, and excellent balance of doctrine and comfort.
